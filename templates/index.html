Hello hello!

I’m Sahiti. Of the many permutations of my name, my favorite is Sahityam (సాహిత్యం). I only recently entered the world of public policy where I try to channel my mild frustrations from walking ill-lit streets, searching for data in developing countries, and navigating patriarchal institutions.

I usually spend my time writing, coding, and playing storytelling games. But I’m most proud of holding 8-hour long conversations, moving apartments on a bicycle, wearing a saree in five different styles, and a handful of amazing friends.

Currently, I’m looking to tell more stories about gender, culture, and society in developing countries. I also want to document and translate oral stories from Indian regional languages*. If you have a story your grandmother handed to you, a secret stash of data waiting to be visualized, or an idea that will let us build a whole new urban mythology, I can’t wait to hear from you. Drop me an email at sahiti.sarva@gmail.com or reach out on Twitter!


*I speak Telugu and Hindi and understand some Tamil. We could document/translate stories from any of them.
