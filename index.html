Hello hello!

I’m Sahiti. Of the many permutations of my name, my favorite is Sahityam (సాహిత్యం). I only recently entered the world of public policy where I try to channel my mild frustrations from walking ill-lit streets, searching for data in developing countries and navigating patriarchal institutions.

I usually spend my time writing, coding, and playing storytelling games. But I’m most proud of holding 8-hour long conversations, moving apartments on a bicycle, wearing a saree in 5 different styles and my friends*.

This is a repository I’m slowly building of the work I enjoyed doing. Currently, I’m looking to tell more stories about gender, culture and society in developing countries and want to document and translate oral stories from Telugu. If you have a story your grandmother handed to you, a secret stash of data waiting to be visualized or an idea that will let us to build a whole new urban mythology+, I can’t wait to hear from you. Drop me an email at sahiti.sarva@gmail.com or reach out on Twitter!
